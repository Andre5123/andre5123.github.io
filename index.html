Meta-moment

This website is part a final project that explores themes and characters in Ruth Ozeki's "A Tale for the Time Being". It's meant to be a modern reinterpretation of my blog website that I abandoned soon after moving to Japan.

The space below is designed to capture both sides of the story‚Äîmine (Nao's) and Ruth's. We communicated with each other across an ocean, across time, through only the words and objects that drifted between our worlds. And now, I get to share my stories with you! Whoever you are.

üìö The situation at school

Okay, remember how I said it would get better? Yeah. about that.

I totally messed up in Japanese class. Like, REALLY screwed up. I was trying to read a passage from a book out loud, and I messed up this one word, and apparently it sounded super dirty? The WHOLE class just erupted in laughter. Even the teacher had to turn away to hide his smile.

By now they figured it out: The dumb gaijin girl makes great entertainment.

At lunch, I went to sit down and someone yanked the chair from under me right as I was about to sit. WHAM. I fell straight on my butt in front of everyone. This bossy girl Reiko goes "Oops! sorry!", acting all sweet and innocent, but I could see it in her eyes. She meant to do it. Scary.

After school, some kids blocked me by the lockers. They started pressing me with questions in super fast Japanese, then cracked up when I couldn't answer. One of them grabbed my uniform and said something like "Aw, the poor migrant can't even afford the right size.

Then I was shoved into a locker.

I seriously don't get it. What did I even do? Besides that one slip up, I don't think I've done anything to deserve being made a target. At this point, I just want to drop out of existence.

The Chuo Rapid Express Incident

I don't even know what day it is anymore

My dad was lying to us the whole time. Oh, and he tried to kill himself today. That's new.

There was no tech startup, no empathy-based productivity software, no high-end job to save us from this crappy appartment.

There's only the 5,000,000 yen fine that we were left with. That's what they make you pay for causing a "Human Incident" at the Chuo Rapid Express line.

Mom picked him up from the police station. We had a family debrief where he basically confessed that everything was a load of bull and that he had been spending his days at the park feeding crows and gambling on horses. Apparently he got drunk and "accidentally" stumbled in front of the train tracks.

When they came home, Dad wouldn't look at me. He just shuffled past like a ghost and locked himself in the bathroom. I could hear Mom crying in their bedroom, trying to muffle the sound with a pillow.

And me? I just sat there. Staring at the wall. Feeling absolutely nothing and everything at the same time.

How am I supposed to go to school tomorrow and deal with my idiotic classmates when my own father wants to not exist? How am I supposed to care about anything when the person who's supposed to protect me can't even protect himself?

I feel so stupid. At least I'm still here. At least I'm still trying.

But honestly? I get it. I understand why he wanted to disappear. Sometimes existing just hurts too much.

What should I do?

Doesn't sound very superhero-ish, right? But here's the thing‚Äîit's WAY harder than it sounds. I've been pretty diligent so far. And honestly? Once you get the hang of it, zazen is kind of peaceful. Like home, if you can call it that. Like I can finally just exist without all the noise in my head telling me how much everything sucks.

Also, we're getting ready for Obon! It's this Buddhist festival where you honor the spirits of your ancestors by throwing them a big party. Our Whaletown Online Journal

Notes from Ruth & Oliver

Lunchbox

March, sometime after the storm

Objects wash up on our beach all the time: plastic bottles, fishing nets, shoes that have traveled thousands of miles (some of them even have dried feet inside). I can't understand how Muriel seems to find joy in scavenging these things... For non-scavengers like me, most of it is garbage.

But sometimes, rarely, I find something neat or inspiring.

We found a teenage girl's diary from a decade ago washed up on the shore tucked inside of a Hello Kitty Lunchbox. Her name is Nao. She writes in purple pen. Round, sloppy, loopy handwriting. Very American, even though I just learned she's writing it from Tokyo.

It's weird, I have this inexplicable desire to try and save her. Ridiculous, I know. It's already been a decade since Nao wrote this. But the whole diary is practically a cry for help! How am I supposed to ignore it?

I should probably try to find her and return it. But I've started reading, and I can't stop.

The Weight of Reading

Late March

I've been reading Nao's diary obsessively. At night, when Oliver is asleep, I sometimes take it to the kitchen table and start tracing her words over with my finger, reading in the dark. Right now, I can tell she's getting scared because her handwriting gets smaller and tighter. Like she's trying to disappear into the page. What's most frustrating is that I can't do anything about it!

I get this really disturbing sense whenever I read about Nao's private pain. Like I've become complicit. I'm a witness who has arrived too late to intervene, who can only watch the drowning happen in slow motion. Still, I need to read on. For Nao. Maybe then I can find a way to throw her a lifejacket.

I keep thinking about the funeral they held for her; the one where she was still alive, watching online as her classmates performed the ceremonial rites and faked their grief for her. She said the view counter kept climbing, up to five hundred.How many of those viewers were laughing? How many felt nothing at all?

I have still have the urge to try reach back through time and help her. Sadly, time doesn't work that way.

Crows

Late March

I've seen that crow floating around the island all week. It's a Jungle Crow, according Oliver. A non-native species from Japan. How did it get here?

Nao writes about crows too. Her father feeds them in the park. Every day, while he's supposed to be job hunting, he sits on a bench and feeds crows. I can't imagine the shame he's living with.

I've been Googling as many keywords as I can to try and find him. "Haruki. Harryki. Haruki Yasutani, Stanford professor, computer engineer". And then nothing. The trail goes cold around 2003.

And the diary! Oliver says I'm obsessing. He's probably right. But what else am I supposed to do? The diary just stops! Mid-sentence, practically! Like she put down her pen and never picked it up again.

I need to know what happened to her. What can I do?
